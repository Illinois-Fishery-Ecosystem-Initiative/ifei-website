Collaborators

Our Partnerships

We work alongside the organizations already doing conservation work in Northern Illinois — providing data they don't have the capacity to collect themselves.

Active Partners

Buffalo Grove Environmental Action Team

Buffalo Creek Watershed

The EAT leads the Buffalo Creek Clean Water Partnership, working to improve water quality across the Buffalo Creek watershed. IFEI is collaborating to provide continuous sensor data to support their monitoring and reporting work.

Contact: Jeff Weiss · environmental@bgparks.org

Flint & Spring Creek Watersheds Partnership

Flint Creek · Spring Creek

The FCSCWP works to improve water quality and preserve ecosystems across the Flint and Spring Creek watersheds. They have expressed active interest in new monitoring solutions to address gaps in their current data collection.

Contact: Karen Lange · kdlange@fcscwp.org

Sierra Club Illinois — Water Sentinels

Statewide · Northern Illinois Focus

The Sierra Club Water Sentinels program mobilizes networks across Illinois around clean water issues. IFEI is coordinating with their Water Sentinel leaders who have existing monitoring programs in our target area.

Contact: Mila Marshall · mila.marshall@sierraclub.org

Lake County Health Department

Lake County

The Lake County Health Department monitors water quality across Lake County waterways for public health, including harmful algal bloom advisories and beach closures. IFEI has been referred to the Executive Director for follow-up.

In Discussion

Lake County Forest Preserves

31,000+ Acres · Multiple Lakes

The largest land manager in our region, overseeing 31,000+ acres including numerous lakes and waterways. Home to sensitive species including Blanding's turtles whose aquatic habitat depends on water quality conditions.

Contact: Gary Glowacki, Manager of Conservation Ecology

Village of Vernon Hills

Big Bear Lake · Little Bear Lake

The Village oversees Environment and Natural Resource Restoration efforts including ongoing work at Harvey Lake and Lake Charles. IFEI has fished Big Bear and Little Bear Lake and is pursuing a deployment partnership.

Contact: Chris Venatta, Director of Public Works

Des Plaines River Watershed Workgroup

Des Plaines River Watershed

The DRWW coordinates monitoring and water quality improvement efforts across the Des Plaines River watershed. IFEI is in contact with the Monitoring and Water Quality Improvements Committee Chair.

Contact: Steve Waters, North Shore Water Reclamation District

North Cook County Soil & Water Conservation District

North Cook County

The NCCSWCD works on conservation and water resource protection across North Cook County. IFEI has reached out to explore how continuous sensor data could complement their existing conservation work.

Contact: Rick McAndless, Resource Conservationist

Interested in Partnering?

We're actively seeking park districts, watershed groups, and conservation organizations in Northern Illinois to deploy sensors at their managed waterways. All data is shared openly with partners at no cost.

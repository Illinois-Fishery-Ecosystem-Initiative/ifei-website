The Problem

Harmful algal blooms close Northern Illinois lakes every summer. Park districts lack the continuous data needed to predict or prevent them — most monitoring is done quarterly at best.

02

Our Approach

We build and deploy low-cost conductivity and turbidity sensors in partnership with local park districts, generating 24/7 data that captures events no quarterly sample ever could.

03

Open Science

All data, firmware, and hardware schematics are published openly on GitHub. Any community can replicate our methodology. We build for the commons, not for profit.

What We Measure

⚡

Conductivity

Tracks dissolved ions in water — a direct indicator of road salt runoff, nutrient loading, and invasive species risk. Zebra mussels require conductivity above 150 µS/cm to survive.

◎

Turbidity

Measures water clarity through light scattering. Combined with conductivity, turbidity fingerprints pollution event types and provides early warning of algal bloom onset.

▲

Continuous Logging

Our sensors log every 15–30 minutes, 24 hours a day. A conductivity spike at 3am after a rainstorm — invisible to quarterly sampling — is captured automatically.

The Device

A sub-$50 solar-powered sensor unit deployable by anyone, anywhere along a shoreline — no dock access, no infrastructure required.

Design Philosophy

Every design decision is driven by three constraints: cost, deployability, and maintainability. A sensor that requires a technician to service it won't scale. Ours can be serviced by anyone who can pull a stake out of the ground.

Cost

Target bill of materials under $50 per unit. Mass produceable. Replicable by any school, watershed group, or park district.

Deployment

Ground spike into any shoreline. No dock access, no permanent installation, no permits for the hardware itself. One person, five minutes.

Maintenance

Monthly SD card swap during a regular site visit. Clean the probe. Done. No wireless infrastructure to manage.

System Architecture

1

Ground Spike

PVC or metal spike driven into the shoreline. Houses the ESP32 microcontroller, SD card module, LiPo battery, and charge controller in a sealed weatherproof enclosure. Solar panel mounts to the top, facing skyward.

→
2

Tether Cable

Thin 4-conductor waterproof cable runs from the spike to the water. Bright colored for visibility. Staked to the ground with tent pegs. Length determines sensor depth — marked with a zip tie for consistent deployment.

→
3

Sensor Sphere

Polycarbonate sphere, openable at the equator, weighted to sink to a fixed depth below the surface. Sensor probes mount through the top hemisphere. No electronics in the water — just the probes themselves.

→
4

Data Collection

ESP32 wakes every 15–30 minutes, reads all sensors, timestamps and logs to SD card, returns to deep sleep. Monthly site visit: pull the spike, swap the SD card, clean the probe, redeploy.

Conductivity Probe

The conductivity probe uses two stainless steel 316-grade electrodes at a fixed, known distance apart. A small AC voltage is applied between the electrodes. The resulting current is measured and used to calculate conductivity.

AC excitation (rather than DC) prevents electrolysis — the primary cause of electrode degradation and reading drift in cheap DIY probes. The fixed electrode spacing defines the cell constant, a calibration factor that converts raw current measurements into conductivity units (µS/cm).

Calibration

One-point calibration using a reference solution of known conductivity. Recalibrated monthly during SD card swap visits.

Biofouling

Algae and bacteria accumulate on electrode surfaces over time. 316 stainless steel resists fouling well. Probes are cleaned during monthly maintenance visits — conveniently timed with SD card retrieval.

Electrode material

316 stainless steel

Excitation

AC signal (prevents electrolysis)

Output

µS/cm (microsiemens per centimeter)

Calibration

Monthly, single-point
